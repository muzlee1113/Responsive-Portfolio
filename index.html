About Me

Growing up in a family of an architect and a civil engineer, I have closely witnessed the value and power of design and technology since childhood. In college, however, I discovered my interest and passion in communication and education. When I thought I would go down a path completely off of my parents, interestingly enough, all the successes and challenges I experienced in my study and work led me to rethink the power and value of design and technology, connecting me back to my family heritage and pointing me towards the job as a UX Designer.

I first became fascinated by the topic of incremental change when I designed the lightweight online reading product that I mentioned earlier. One goal of the product was to cultivate students’ habits in reading and learning. In designing product features to achieve the goal, I came across some interesting challenges and was motivated to explore behavior change theories such as Charles Duhigg’s habit loop and BJ Fogg’s motivation wave. First, in light of Charles Duhigg’s theory that a habit forms through a loop propelled by continuous rewards, I utilized gamified rewards such as ranking, trophy, and growing avatar, into my design of the reading product to sustain students’ reading actions. Second, inspired by BJ Fogg’s theory that a designer should not rely on forcefully boosted motivation but harness whatever motivation exists at the moment by assigning tasks of best-suited difficulty levels (hardest task for high motivation moment and vice versa) to drive behavior change, I envisioned in my design of the reading product to tap into user context data and behavior data to recognize different students’ motivation levels at different times and give tasks accordingly. Of course, such feature relies on personalization technology to make it happen, which is another exciting area to explore that can significantly empower product and solution design

Designing to increase information relevance is another area of my major interest. I started to realize the issue of information relevance when I worked at New Oriental, a private educational service provider. As the largest of its kind in China, the institution in Beijing alone offers more than 300 different English courses of varying levels, topics, teaching methods, etc. Finding the right course that fits each individual student is challenging even with the aid of the course search engine and the counselors; a lot of assumptions about the student’s abilities have to be made and many trials and errors are needed. Throughout the time managing the course, I accumulated a large pool of student engagement and performance data, which unfortunately were not fully exploited. I see a lot of potentials in leveraging data technology to offer a more personalized and thus more effective learning experience. For example, I have always envisioned to create and maintain digital student profiles to closely track students’ learning trajectory and therefrom utilize adaptive learning technology to provide dynamically adjusted content throughout students’ learning journey.
